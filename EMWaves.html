EM waves

EM waves or electromagnetic waves are those caused by photons of a electromagnetic field carrying electromagnetic radiant energy. This causes electromagnetic radiation. There are 7 main types of EM waves. There are other types of these waves but we will learn only these 7 types of waves.

Terms of EM waves

There are terms to begin with learning about EM waves. They are as follows.
  • Frequency: The frequency of a wave is the number of times per second that the wave cycles. Frequency is commonly measured in Hertz.
  • Wavelength: The wavelength of a wave is the distance between two corresponding points on back-to-back cycles of a wave. Wavelength is usually represented in physics by the Greek letter lambda (Î»).

Types of EM waves

Radio waves

Radio waves have a long wavelength but short frequency. They are used in radar, sonar, working of radioes, etc. They can pass through certain objects like ice but can get blocked by others like clouds and rain.

Microwaves

Microwaves are a little like radio waves but have shorter wavelength and higher frequency. They are obviously used in microwave ovens and also in Wi-Fi and phones. Interesting! They are blocked by metals and pass through paper,glass and plastics.

Infrared waves

Infrared waves have shorter wavelength than microwaves but have an even higher frequency. There are many uses of infrared waves and they are included in infrared cameras, remote controls,etc. They pass through dense reigons of gas and are blocked by certain substances like plexiglass.
